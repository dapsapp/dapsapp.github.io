Why a QR scan feels like an autograph.
When an athlete signs something for you, they extend their wrist. You receive it. That gesture is the whole thing.

DAPS recreates that gesture digitally. The athlete wears a QR wristband — visible, always on, just like their normal wristbands and headbands. When they activate a window, they're choosing to give. When you point your camera at their wrist up close, you're receiving.

The QR being visible doesn't mean it works. A scan from across the room fails. A screenshot from a broadcast fails. You had to be right there — close enough that they could look you in the eye. That proximity is the autograph.

The athlete doesn't need new habits. Their assistant activates it the same way they post to Instagram. The wristband is already on their wrist.
The moment was real.
Now you have proof.
